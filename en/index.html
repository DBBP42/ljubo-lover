About Us

Why this project exists

Sexual violence and exploitation often remain invisible, especially when the victims are men or LGBT+ individuals.

The project aims to break myths, reduce stigma, and offer clear information.

The goal is not to shock, but to empower.

Education

How to recognize abuse

Consent

Consent must be clear, conscious, and can be withdrawn at any time. Silence or intoxication is not consent.

Manipulation

Emotional pressure, blackmail, guilt-tripping, or threats are forms of abuse.

Control

Isolation from friends, monitoring communication, or threats of exposure are red flags.

Substances

Using alcohol or drugs to reduce resistance or memory means consent is absent.

Risks

Chemsex – risks and reality

Chemsex involves using substances to enhance or facilitate sexual activity. While it can be a choice, it is often used as a tool for exploitation.

Important: Intoxication is not consent. If you were unable to say no, it was not your fault.

Understanding Trauma

Trauma is not just a memory; it is a physical and psychological response to overwhelming events. Your body's reaction (freeze, fawn, etc.) was a survival mechanism.
