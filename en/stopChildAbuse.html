Prevention & Action

Stop Child Abuse

Child sexual abuse is a global crisis that thrives in silence and ignorance. Breaking that silence is the first step toward a safer world for every child.

The Reality

Understanding the scope

Prevalence

Statistics show that 1 in 5 children will experience some form of sexual violence before the age of 18. This is not a "rare" event.

The Perpetrators

In over 90% of cases, the perpetrator is someone the child knows and trusts—a family member, a teacher, a coach, or a family friend.

The Impact

Unaddressed trauma from childhood abuse can lead to lifelong struggles with mental health, addiction, and physical illness.

The Cost of Silence

When we don't talk about abuse, we protect the perpetrators and leave the victims to suffer in isolation.

What We Can Do

Taking action

Prevention is a collective responsibility. We must create a culture where children are believed and perpetrators are held accountable.

  • Education: Teach children about bodily autonomy and the difference between "good" and "bad" secrets.
  • Training: Ensure that all professionals working with children are trained to recognize the signs of abuse.
  • Legislation: Advocate for stronger laws, longer statutes of limitations, and better victim protection.
  • Support: Fund and support organizations that provide specialized care for child survivors and their families.

Every child deserves safety

We cannot change the past, but we can change the future. By speaking out and taking action, we can create a world where children are safe to grow, play, and thrive.
